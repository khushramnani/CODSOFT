TIMELINE:

  • February 17, 1975: David Goggins is born in Buffalo, New York.
  • Early 2000s: Goggins joins the U.S. Navy SEALs, undergoing the grueling BUD/S (Basic Underwater Demolition/SEAL) training.
  • 2005:Goggins begins participating in ultra-marathons and endurance races, despite initially disliking running.
  • 2006:Completes his first ultra-marathon, the San Diego One Day, where he ran 101 miles.
  • 2007:Sets a world record for the most pull-ups done in 24 hours, completing 4,030 pull-ups.
  • 2008:Attempts to compete in the Badwater-135, a 135-mile ultra-marathon through Death Valley, but is forced to drop out due to health issues.
  • 2010:Completes the Badwater-135 ultra-marathon, despite suffering from severe physical issues during the race.
  • 2013:Publishes his memoir, "Can't Hurt Me: Master Your Mind and Defy the Odds," which becomes a bestseller.
  • 2015:Sets the Guinness World Record for the most pull-ups done in 24 hours, completing 4,030 pull-ups.
  • 2016: Completes the "Cocodona 250" ultra-marathon, a 250-mile race through Arizona.
  • 2018: Begins the "Four by 4 by 48 Challenge," running 4 miles every 4 hours for 48 hours, inspiring thousands to join him in the challenge..
  • 2020: Continues to inspire and motivate people worldwide through his social media platforms, sharing his philosophy of mental toughness and pushing beyond limits.
